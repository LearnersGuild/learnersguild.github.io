Do You Dream to Be a Developer?
We’ll Invest in You.

You’ve done school. You’ve seen the online courses. You’ve checked out the coding bootcamps. And still, you’ve dreamed of something more.

So did we.

If you’re self-motivated, curious, collaborative… there’s a place for you to build in-depth skills as a software engineer. And to impact your life for the better, while you’re at it.

Give us 40 weeks to challenge, guide, and support you in the most engaging learning experience you’ve ever had. Our methods are backed by cutting-edge learning research. Self-driven so you can move at your own pace. Collective so you can learn deep. And it’s fun. Serious levels of smart, but stupid levels of fun. In the end, you walk out the door with a new career, new opportunities, and new friends for life.

And, with a little luck, it doesn’t end there. Because to us, it’s not just about giving you a shot at a dignified career. It’s also about hacking the emerging culture of this powerful new industry to become more inclusive (and, we think, a bit happier). We hope you’ll join us.

So, then... if that’s you and you’re up for growing, we’d like to invest in you.

We’ll co-sign your $28k tuition up-front, and offer you a $2k monthly living allowance while you attend. (Let’s face it, it’s hard to focus on learning if you’re also struggling to cover the bills.) The kicker? If you graduate and don’t earn at least a $50k salary, you pay us nothing.

Why We're Different

Learning: The Difference.

Getting an education and learning sound the like same thing. But that’s not quite true: education is knowledge given to you, while learning is knowledge you give to yourself. Educations expire. Learning, doesn’t.

It’s a harsh truth of technology that what you’re taught today will be obsolete tomorrow. And if you’re going to gain in-depth skills as a software engineer, then you’re going to want to up your skills at learning. And that’s one of the big things that sets Learners Guild apart: we strive to be a place to explore, share discoveries, and learn-- really learn-- the evolving art of software development. So it sounds like an education, but learning is so different, we think it could change your life in a nice way..

Another big thing that makes us different? Well, we think it’s big... but it’s a delicate subject. It’s about race, gender, and orientation. That stuff matters to us. Deeply. We’re passionate about creating an inclusive learning environment. If you’re from an historically underrepresented group (in terms of software developers) then we really want you in here. Black, Latino, Pacific Islander, LGBT, Women… we’ve designed our program with you in mind. And yes, White guys, we want you too. Truly. We want “the Great Plurality” of people. We want everyone.

Learn more

40 Weeks of Wow.

Learn how to make a dignified and soulful living as a full-stack web developer.

How?

Here’s where it gets exciting. As we’ve said, we’re not a school. Schools are a teaching environment. And we’re a learning environment. We see knowledge and skills as something to be explored. Traditional models suggest knowledge is simply information that must be passed down from teachers to students. It suggests that neither teachers nor students can learn from each other, yet we know that’s not true. In fact, it’s the opposite. Research shows that by exploring together, we all learn more, and we learn faster.

Learners Guild help you build your abilities to tackle new challenges, and gives you the chance to test your skills. You have the freedom to learn what you want, when you want, with a supportive group of peers who share similar goals. And our expert facilitators are there to help you choose your path of exploration, and to help you if you get stuck.

But there’s so much more to it than just learning engineering skills. Software development doesn’t happen in a vacuum. You still have to work with other humans, and social dynamics matter. Developers who feel like strangers in a strange land, or don’t know how to skillfully navigate difficult team dynamics, will often leave their engineering career behind. This is particularly true for historically underrepresented groups of engineers. Because a lack of EQ results in reduced learning and limits in one’s ability to advance into leadership roles, Learners Guild addresses this head-on in our program. Computers follow logic. Humans, don’t. Learners Guild provides tools to help you excel at working with both the world of machines, and the world of people.

Isn’t “Learning Collective” just a fancy way of saying you’re a school or boot camp?

No. The top-down (teacher-student) approach embraced by most educational systems is out-dated, slow, and inefficient at best. Historically, bootcamps have a short-term vision aimed at helping students enter an industry.

Learners Guild, however, has a long-range vision that goes far beyond simply making the leap into software engineering. We want you to be set up for a rapidly advancing, extended career. Our program is designed around a cognitive apprenticeship method of learning-- one that has been vetted by scientists and learning researchers-- that has shown itself to be a truly powerful means of both knowledge acquisition and skill development.

Because we use a “mutual exploration” (learners-only) model, and we believe in the effectiveness of this approach, we invest in our learners. It’s part of the collaborative spirit of exploring. If you don’t get paid well after graduating, you don’t owe us. We share the risk with you.

Learners-only? Does that mean there aren’t teachers?

Yes. Learning and teaching are different, and we focus on learning. While we certainly have people with in-depth experience, senior level engineers, skilled facilitators, and so on, we don’t have teachers. A well designed learning environment causes a rapid transfer of knowledge from peers. As you learn, you will share what you know. This is a kind of “mutual exploration” of the knowledge. Trying to explain what you know challenges you to deepen your own understanding, even as you assist someone else with theirs.

Because technology changes so quickly, knowing information isn’t enough. It doesn’t take long for today’s right answers to become obsolete. To prepare you, our learning facilitators focus on improving your problem-solving skills. Essentially, you learn how to learn. This way, you’re equipped with the skills that make life-long learning attainable, something that’s critical to becoming a good developer.

Will we be graded?

Testing your understanding and skills? Definitely. But grades? No. There’s a big difference. Grading is a carrot-and-stick approach that assumes you must be coerced to learn. We’re not here to coerce. We only accept applicants who have a drive to learn. As learners, we don’t focus on grades. Instead, we focus on improving our understanding and skills. Essentially: can you can build something to do exactly what you want it to do? Yes? Then the grade doesn’t matter.

What will we learn?

Over a period of about 40 weeks, you’ll have the chance to learn everything you need to know to become a truly savvy full-stack web developer. You can find a list of graduating skillsets, HERE.

A $2,000/month Stipend? Really?

Yes. Really. We want you to focus on learning, not earning. A $2,000 monthly stipend allows you to make learning your full-time job for 10 months. Remember, if you don’t succeed, then we don’t. So we take our investment in you seriously.

What does this cost? How does the money work? If you’re investing in me, how do you get paid?

Good questions. A commitment to Learners Guild, is a full-time commitment. We want our learners to spend most of those 10 months focused on honing their craft. As a result, Learners Guild offers a stipend of up to $2000 a month for learners who may need it.

The cost of attending Learners Guild is $28,000. If a learner takes the maximum of the additional stipend, that increases their cost by an additional $20,000, bring the total to $48,000. Once a learner has transitioned out into the workforce with earnings over $50,000, they start to pay back their cost of tuition and stipend. Learners Guild makes money by receiving the cost of tuition, while we put the stipend back in a pool, for other learners to use.

Typical starting salaries are $85k-$95k a year. Learners have the option of paying back the loan in installments over a five year period, with an average payment of $500 a month for tuition and an additional $340 if the maximum amount of monthly stipends are used.

What happens if I quit the process before graduating?

It’s in our best interest to see you thrive. We’re very careful to choose applicants who are committed to the process, and if you’re committed to learning, we’re determined to give you all the support you need to finish. In fact, that’s one reason we allow you to take extra time if necessary. That all said, if you opt out, you will still owe the full tuition, and any stipends you’ve received. (On a case-by-case basis, we are willing to pause the program for those who face extenuating circumstances.)

What are the requirements to attend?

Beyond a common desire to become a software engineer in a mutually supportive environment... perhaps first and foremost, all of our learners share a drive to learn and grow. Not just intellectually, but socially and emotionally as well. We expect a minimum level of personal development and maturity, as (Our learners are open to personal transformation.)

Be accountable. Curious. And committed. We like to see some demonstration of those qualities, usually through some basic employment, volunteer, or other such history. And of course, the availability to participate full-time for 40 weeks.

Finally, we do look for basic technical interest and exposure to programming. Ideally, you will have spent some time fiddling with html, and some basic exposure to programming concepts. Ever heard of variables and functions? For loops? Arrays or lists? If those terms are unfamiliar, then we’d encourage you to take a week to play around with the basics. The important thing is to give yourself some exposure to programming before committing to 40 weeks of intense exploration of the subject.

What kind of people will I be learning with?

Other people that share a drive to collaborate and learn. So, people just like you. Only, from a wide range of backgrounds. And in different shapes and sizes.

Will I end up with a portfolio?

Yes, you’ll have an excellent opportunity to work on projects that will add to your portfolio. Some smaller, some larger, team-assembled projects. (This kind of diversity looks good in a portfolio, btw.)

Will the skills I learn be the in-demand skills I’ll need to be a Full Stack Developer?

In short, yes. But it’s more than learning specific technologies. Learners Guild gives you a few distinct skill advantages. One of them is the ability to boost your own learning. Today’s answers will become obsolete. This is true of life, but it’s particularly true for technology fields. Knowing how to stay on top of the knowledge curve is something that allows ordinary engineers to excel and take on leadership roles.

In addition, we give you the soft-skills. Those squishy, human-centered, social skills are another big differentiator between junior developers, and team leaders.

Do you have a remote/online option?

No. Research shows that learning in a live, group context is accelerated. You learn more, you learn faster, and the depth of your understanding goes deeper.

Isn’t this too good to be true?

You’ll probably never stop asking this question. (We wish we existed when we started our careers.) In fact, it’s our goal that you ask this every day you show up and find yourself learning in ways you never thought possible. We want you to ask this 40 weeks later, when you’ve landed a job you didn’t think you could have. And we want you to ask this 5, 10, and 20 years later… when you’re still in touch, and still getting support from the friends you made at Learners Guild.

Do you have a shorter immersion program?

Currently, no. While it’s possible to cover the basics in a shorter course, our goal is to have you walk out with a much deeper skillset than “just the basics.” If we find a way to streamline our approach and still produce stellar results, we’ll certainly consider it.

Do I need my own computer?

No. We have Mac computers set up for you using the latest version of Mac OS.

Can I visit your campus to check it out?

Not currently. We’re protective of our learners. However, you can meet some of them and our team by showing up at one of our hackathons.

Will I earn a certificate or a degree?

No. Our approach is strictly learning-oriented. There’s a lot of people with degrees, but no job to match it. It’s a common mistake to confuse a symbol for learning with the actual learning, itself. Learners Guild turns that around: if you don’t end up in a high-paying job, we don’t get paid. We’d rather you had a dignified career making good money. (Save your would-be-diploma picture frame for the reunion photo with your LG friends.)

Ready to Level Up?

We’re not gonna lie. We’re excited. We want to meet you and get you involved. There’s a couple of ways to do this. You can call us. Email us. Text us. Instagram us. Whatever. But that just means more time between now and the moment we get to meet.

So why not just stop by? We’re doing hackathon days coming up and you should join us. Don’t know what a hackathon is? Sounds scary? It's not. It' just a bunch of good people who like to tinker and learn coding stuff. Basically, the opposite of scary.

And if you can’t swing by, or just need an LG application form, or have other questions, then email or call us.
