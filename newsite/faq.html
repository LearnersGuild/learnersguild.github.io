What's a guild?

A guild is an old idea, set to be the future of learning.

In the distant past, if you wanted to learn a trade, you'd join a guild. It wasn't theoretical, it was experential. Young apprentices would work under proved masters, each generation investing in and giving back to the one that followed. Guilds not only kept their crafts alive, they formed a society of mutual help and companionship.

A guild is an old idea, set to be the future of learning.

Today's guilds are learning communities entirely different from typical schools. If you want to take on all the financial risk and go it alone, you go to a college or a bootcamp. If you want to be part of a community that loves its craft and those who practice it, if you want to be lifted by others, and to lift others with you, you join a guild.

How the Money Works

Good Questions. We know it's unusual, but program fees for Learners Guild do not have to be paid upfront. People who want to, can pay the upfront price of $29,750 for the program, but most people opt to pay for the Learners Guild program through an Income Sharing Agreement (ISA), where you will contribute 12.5% of your salary (or up to 21% if you receive a living stipend) for the first three years’ of employment. The typical starting salary for a junior web developer / software engineer is $75K to $95K per year. As a Public Benefit Corporation, we want to make Learners Guild accessible to everyone, no matter their ability to pay upfront fees. This means we’re quite literally investing in you: we want you to get an awesome high-paying job, and we only get paid if you succeed. We’re betting on you and our ability to help you reach your potential, so if you don’t make over $50,000 after your time at the Guild, we don’t make a dime.

In general terms, an Income Share Agreement (ISA) is a contractual agreement in which an individual receives funding in exchange for an agreed upon percentage of post-program income over a defined number of years. At Learners Guild we offer 2 ISAs, one that covers the program fees and another that covers the provision of the living allowance, both are optional. How much funding is available for individual learners? Individual funding amounts vary depending whether a Learner elects to enter into an ISA for the program fees, and/or an ISA for living allowance (which can also cover the cost of a laptop). The ISA for the program fees covers the entire $29,750 funding amount for the program and requires an income share of 12.5% for 3 years, once you complete the program. And, the ISA for the living allowance, allows the Learner to receive up to $1,500 / month (which equates to $346.15/week) to pay for living expenses -- rent, computer, food, etc.-- during the program in exchange for up to 8.5% of income for 3 years.

Yes, really. The $1,500 monthly living allowance is intended to help defray your cost of living while you're at Learners Guild. As much as possible, we want you to focus on learning, not earning. Learners can elect to enter into an ISA for up to $1,500/month in living allowance, but it is totally optional and you can take less or none if you don’t need the full amount. $1,500/month equates to $346.15/week and there is also an option to receive an additional $1,846 to cover the cost of a laptop. Payments for the living allowance are made to Learners every 2 weeks, in advance, starting on the second Friday of the program.

The Learners Guild ISAs offer learners an alternative to taking on debt to cover the program fee and/or debt to cover living expenses. Debt creates substantial risks if the individual cannot afford the payments, whereas ISA payments adjust according to levels of income. In addition, the ISA payments for Learners Guild are subject to a minimum income threshold of $50,000, and a maximum payment cap equal to two (2) times the amount funded, so students who opt into the ISA will not pay if they do not meet the minimum income threshold, while those who earn a substantial amount of income will not pay above the maximum capped amount

The ISA allows Learners to have an option not to have to pay the program fees upfront. In addition to making Learners Guild accessible to anyone, regardless of financial means, we also like it because it aligns our success with our Learners. We only succeed and are able to invest in future Learners if people make it through our program, get a well-paying job, and make payments on their ISAs. Finally, we feel ISAs are an alternative funding option for Learners, one that could reduce debt and financial risk.

Your Commitment

In order to be admitted to Learners Guild, applicants must meet all of the requirements listed.

  • Committed to pursuing a full-time career as a web developer / software engineer
  • Willing to aid others in their learning
  • Age 21 or older
  • Oral and written proficiency in English
  • Has several years' employment and/or community service experience
  • Participate full-time, in person, (m-f, 8:30am to 6:00pm) for 40-weeks
  • Can type at least 30 words per minute
  • Must be US citizen or permanent resident
  • Current and anticipated financial obligations at a reasonable threshold

Beyond a common desire to become a software engineer, all of our Learners share a drive to learn and grow in a mutually supportive environment, not just intellectually, but socially and emotionally as well. We expect a minimum level of personal development and maturity. We look for a baseline proficiency in both technical and social-emotional aptitude. It is important to understand that Learners Guild is not designed to support persons who are exploring programming for the first time. We are a learning collective that works best for advanced beginners. If you are just starting to explore programming, you should take advantage of the many freely available resources for beginners so that you can be best prepared. We recommend Shay Howe’s HTML & CSS lessons, the Intro to JS course on Khan Academy, and the Make a Website and Make an Interactive Website tracks on Codecademy.

Learners Guild is a full time commitment. 8.30 AM to 6.00 PM, Monday through Friday. Learners who want to put in a few more hours have the option of arriving as early as 7am and staying as late at 10pm. But we all agree to be onsite between 8:30 am - 6:00 pm, and while we want you to make the most of your time at the Guild, we also believe it is important to live a balanced life.

We'll take off one week for winter holiday season/new year's, and another week during the summer. If your time at the Guild crosses over any of these weeks you will have time off, otherwise we expect you not to take vacations during your 40 weeks.

We have a great office space in Old Oakland, centrally located and accessible by public transportation.

Learning Model

At Learners Guild we follow the principles of cognitive apprenticeship to design a system for effective skills training. Much of all learning at the Guild takes place in team projects, similar to the way that actual software applications are built. The essence of our model is that instead of many people learning only from one person, every learner is learning from other learners and, in turn, passing on their skills and knowledge. There will always be "experts" who you can ask the most difficult questions, but in a lot of cases you will be learning from other, more experienced Learners. Because new Learners start every week, there is always a range of skills and experience in the room, and we’ll help you use this range to your advantage. Our system also incorporates game-based learning so you receive stats and feedback to let you track your progress, and let you know where to focus your efforts to improve. Each week you choose projects and goals that are both relevant and challenging, so that you're always working with others on interesting problems that push you into your "Proximal Zone of Development". Each team is assigned a more-experienced team lead who guides and supports the project and the team's learning.

While bootcamps do a decent job at helping students enter the software industry, we think we can do better at setting Learners up for rapidly advancing careers and longer term success. At Learners Guild everyone is both a student and a teacher. While we certainly have people with in-depth industry experience, senior level engineers, and skilled facilitators, we don’t have “teachers”. You'll be collaborating every day with people ahead of you in the program, and people who started after you. As you learn, you'll share what you know. At Learners Guild, knowledge spreads like wildfire. A well-designed learning environment causes a rapid transfer of knowledge between experts and peers. Because technology changes so quickly, knowing information isn’t enough. It doesn’t take long for today’s right answers to be obsolete tomorrow. To prepare you, we focus not only on what you learn, but how you learn it, developing meta-learning and problem-solving skills. Your ability to continue growing and refining your craft will set you up for sustained success as a software developer. Learners Guild graduates are lifelong learners.

We invest in you to learn as much as you need to attain and sustain a career as a software engineer. To support you, we hire expert practitioners to help you craft the best possible learning path towards your goal. If you're looking for a set curriculum like the one you would find at a school or bootcamp, you won't find it here. We don't believe in a cookie-cutter approach to learning. Instead, we focus on two things: helping you learn how to learn, and providing a rich environment for you to practice and develop your craft with other motivated learners. We believe the best answer to "what should I learn?" is "whatever the most skilled people around you know." So if you are surrounded by concert pianists, you're better off studying piano than the violin. We hire skilled full-stack JavaScript web developers to serve as expert practitioners and mentors from whom you can learn skills and receive invaluable guidance. So... will you learn languages like JavaScript? And databases like PostgreSQL and MongoDB? And HTML and CSS and React and git and... The answer is: yes, of course. You certainly will learn these things, and many many more. More importantly, though, you will learn the habits of mind of an engineer. You will learn how to build products with a team. You will learn how to evaluate your own skills and choose appropriate challenges to help you advance them. You will learn how to mentor other learners, not just because it's a Good Thing To Do, but also because the best way to test your knowledge is to teach it. Finally, you will learn how to learn. After all, is there any more important skill?

There are plenty of great coding languages out there, and while JavaScript isn't the only option, it is a solid choice for people starting their careers in software. Here’s why: (1) JavaScript is ubiquitous on the web and expanding rapidly to other platforms, so demand for JS developers is unlikely to stop growing anytime soon; (2) the JavaScript community is robust and there are lots of amazing resources for continued learning and development; (3) and finally, JavaScript is (relatively) simple and straightforward.

Application Process (AP)

Learners Guild requires that you have at least 50 hours of experience in programming. We do this to ensure that you understand enough about the craft to be committed to it. We expect our community members to be passionate about programming and know enough to want to have a meaningful career as a software developer. If you have fewer than 50 hours of programming experience we recommend you check out these resources and come back to completing your application once you’ve accrued the hours: Complete "Make a Website", "Make an Interactive Website", and "Learn the Command Line", "Learn SQL", and "JavaScript" tracks on Codecademy: https://www.codecademy.com/learn/all Complete free Git courses on Code School: https://www.codeschool.com/learn Go through the HTML & CSS course on Shay Howe’s site http://learn.shayhowe.com/html-css/ Plus as many other projects and tutorials as you like! There are lots of other resources and ideas out there. Follow your interests.

At Learners Guild you’ll meet other adults like yourself, from a wide range of backgrounds, who share your dedication, passion, and curiosity. Check out the videos on the site and in our Community section to see some of the current Learners at the Guild.

So, the whole process from submitting your application to receiving your acceptance notification can take 4-8 weeks.
