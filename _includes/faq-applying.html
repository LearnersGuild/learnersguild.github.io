Application Process

Learners Guild requires that you have at least 50 hours of experience in programming. We do this to ensure that you understand enough about the craft to be committed to it. We expect our community members to be passionate about programming and know enough to want to have a meaningful career as a software developer. If you have fewer than 50 hours of programming experience we recommend you check out these resources and come back to completing your application once you’ve accrued the hours: Complete "Make a Website", "Make an Interactive Website", and "Learn the Command Line", "Learn SQL", and "JavaScript" tracks on Codecademy: https://www.codecademy.com/learn/all Complete free Git courses on Code School: https://www.codeschool.com/learn Go through the HTML & CSS course on Shay Howe’s site http://learn.shayhowe.com/html-css/ Plus as many other projects and tutorials as you like! There are lots of other resources and ideas out there. Follow your interests.

At Learners Guild you’ll meet other adults like yourself, from a wide range of backgrounds, who share your dedication, passion, and curiosity. Check out the videos on the site and in our Community section to see some of the current Learners at the Guild.

There are three more steps in our application process: 1) Once you submit an application, if you have met all of the requirements, you will receive an invitation to schedule a video interview with a member of The Guild. We typically have interview slots available within two weeks of when your application is qualified. This interview is focused on fitness with our community culture and with our learning model. 2) After that initial interview, we will let you know within 24 hours if you are being invited to take the Tech Quiz. And don’t worry, it’s a quiz and not a test, so you can take it more than once, if needed. 3) Once you pass the Tech Quiz, you'll be invited to an onsite Enrollment Game where you will meet in person with other applicants, current Learners, and Learners Guild staff. The Enrollment Game mimics what it's like to learn at The Guild - it gives you the opportunity to try out our learning model and some of our cultural practices, and it’s also a good way for us to assess if you are good fit for the program. We currently host Enrollment Games every two weeks. After the Enrollment Game, we inform candidates if they have been accepted into The Guild within one week. So, the whole process from submitting your application to receiving your acceptance notification can take 4-8 weeks.

You will execute 1 or 2 ISAs, depending on if you opt to not pay up front fees and/or take a living allowance stipend. The ISAs are issued by subsidiaries of Learners Guild. Also, you should be aware that another company, VEMO Education (www.vemo.com), will be responsible administering the ISAs and collecting payments.
