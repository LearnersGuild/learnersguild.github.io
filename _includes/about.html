Proposal #1: No Upfront Costs.

How can you pay for an education if you haven't earned a dime yet?

At Learners Guild, we don't expect you to pay us until after you’ve completed the program--and only if you're working as a software developer.

Learn More

Proposal #2: No Loan Payments.

Our success is tied to your success.

We don’t do student loans because as a Public Benefit Corporation, we believe you should repay us only if we’ve helped you walk into a successful career in software development.

We operate under an Income Sharing Agreement: you’ll repay us through a small portion of your salary for the first three years of your new career. If you don’t earn an income as a software developer, neither do we.

Learn More

Proposal #3: No Sacrifices

Learning a new trade shouldn't be at the cost of your well-being or your livelihood.

We want your time with Learners Guild to be immersive without being nerve-wracking. That's why we meet weekdays from 8:30am to 6:00 pm, so you can spend time with family, get some rest, and return refreshed and ready to learn.

You'll also have the the opportunity to borrow up to $1500 per month to help cover your costs during your time in the program. We want you to bring your undivided focus and passion to your work with Learners Guild, without stretching yourself beyond your means.

Learn More

Proposal #4: No Catch.

You shouldn’t be made to guess about the total cost of your education.

So here's the deal: You'll repay Learners Guild through an Income Sharing Agreement. We get no more than 20% of your salary for the first three years of your new software developer career.

That's it: no smoke and mirrors, no hidden fees, no program fees. We believe the cost of a great education should be directly linked to great outcomes.
