Computational Thinking

Computational thinking is the range of cognitive abilities needed to program a computer. It includes abilities like logical reasoning, algorithm design, pattern recognition, and evaluating a series of sequential instructions.

Why is computational thinking important? Writing programs for computers can be difficult, requiring significant mental effort. Developing good problem-solving strategies and habits of mind will help you to find solutions more quickly and (hopefully) enjoy the challenge of doing so!

The puzzles here are designed to exercise key computational thinking abilities. They don't require any special technical training beyond basic math. If you enjoy this kind of problem solving, you're likely going to enjoy other aspects of programming.

Puzzle 2: Sequence evaluation

You control the triangle in the grid graph pictured here. Your objective is to collect all of the gold stars by moving the triangle over them.

You can send it 3 kinds of commands: MOVE will make it more forward one square, TURNL will make it turn left, and TURNR will make it turn right.

Which of the following sequences of commands will make the triangle cross over every gold star?

  1. TURNR, 4x MOVE, TURNL, 3x MOVE, TURNL, 7x MOVE, TURNR, 4x MOVE
  2. 7x MOVE, TURNL, 2x MOVE, TURNL, 3x MOVE, TURNL, 6x MOVE, TURNR, 3x MOVE
  3. 3x MOVE, TURNL, 2x MOVE, TURNR, 4x MOVE, TURNR, 5x MOVE, TURNR, 6x MOVE, TURNL, 1x MOVE
  4. 7x MOVE, TURNL, 2x MOVE, TURNL, 4x MOVE, TURNL, 6x MOVE, TURNR, 2x MOVE

Puzzle 3: Conditionals and logical reasoning

A grocery store uses stickers to categorize and label their goods according to the rules listed here.

Read the rules and then answer the questions.

What would the sticker look like for an imported organic banana that can be purchased with food stamps, but is not on sale?

  1. Green square with #i on the label
  2. Green circle with #i on the label
  3. Blue circle with #L on the label
  4. Green circle with I on the label

What kind of sticker would you use for conventional salmon raised locally that are on sale and available for purchase with food stamps?

Write down your answer so you can review later.

  • Rules for labeling grocery items:
  • For fresh produce (fruits and vegetables), the stickers are circular. For meat, fish, and poultry, the stickers are square. For all other products, the stickers are diamond-shaped.
  • The stickers for organic food are green; for conventional food they are blue.
  • Items that are available for purchase with food stamps have a # printed on their stickers.
  • If the food was produced locally, the sticker will have an l. If it was produced in another state in the US, it will have the letter u. Imported items are demarcated by an i on the sticker.
  • If the item is on sale, all letters on the sticker will be printed in uppercase.

Are you Ready to Apply?

If you are new to programming, you may not know whether or not you have the requisite skills to join Learners Guild. Use this self-assessment to help find out.

There is no test in our application—this is purely for you to know if you're ready to apply.

To be ready for the program, you'll need to gain some basic proficiency in two key skills: computational thinking and web development.

Use the exercises below to help you determine if you are ready and to discover resources that can help you prepare.

Once you've finished, you will know if you're ready to submit your application to Learners Guild.

If you are not quite ready, we've listed some freely available resources that you can use to learn. We also recommend seeking out help from other people; software development is a team sport, after all. One place to start is to search for "beginner programmer" or "web development" Meetups in your area.

Reasons you may need to prepare

  • To build the basic skills necessary to fully engage with our enrollment process and to start the program itself
  • To spend a suffient amount of time with programming to know that you like it, and know that you’re ready to commit to a career as a software developer
