Computational thinking is the range of cognitive abilities needed to program a computer. It includes abilities like logical reasoning, algorithm design, pattern recognition, and evaluating a series of sequential instructions.

To self-assess your computational thinking skills, complete the Computer Programming Aptitude Test from the University of Kent. If your score is higher than 14 or so, you’re definitely ready for Learners Guild.

If your score is lower, you will want to practice more to build up your computational thinking ability. Don't be discouraged if you didn't get the score you'd hoped! Computational thinking is a skill just like basketball or dance: the more you practice, the better you’ll get.

*Note: don’t limit yourself to just the above resources! There are a lot more out there if you do some searching.
